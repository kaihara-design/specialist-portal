## Design Context

### Users
Medical specialists (radiologists, dermatologists, pathologists) being onboarded to label medical images for Centaur.ai. They are professionals — not casual users — accessing the portal when invited to a new task. Context of use: primarily desktop/laptop in a clinical or home-office setting, but increasingly on mobile for quick status checks and document signing.

### Brand Personality
Professional, trustworthy, clean. Centaur.ai sits at the intersection of clinical medicine and modern AI infrastructure — the interface should feel like a premium SaaS tool used by experts, not a consumer app. Three words: **precise, credible, modern**.

### Aesthetic Direction
Clean white surfaces, minimal decoration, indigo/violet as the single brand accent. Typography-led hierarchy. No glassmorphism, no dark mode (primary). Layouts feel purposeful — open space is intentional, not empty. Closest reference: Linear or Notion — tools that serious professionals trust.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — specialists are busy; every screen should have one obvious next action
2. **Trust through restraint** — legal documents and medical workflows demand visual sobriety; decoration undermines credibility
3. **Progress is visible** — multi-step flows always show where the user is and what remains
4. **Density when appropriate** — compact data tables and lists are fine; excessive whitespace in data contexts wastes time
5. **Accessible by default** — medical professionals may use screen readers or keyboard navigation; all interactive elements must be focusable and labeled

### Stack
Next.js 16, React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS v4 (CSS variables, no tailwind.config.ts), shadcn/ui new-york neutral, Inter font, lucide-react icons. Primary: `#4f46e5` / `#615fff`. Radius base: 0.5rem (8px). `btn-shadow` utility for primary CTAs.
